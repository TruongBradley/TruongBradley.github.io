Common Car World Terminology

Term Definition
ABS (Anti-lock Braking System) A safety system that prevents wheel lockup during hard braking, maintaining steering control and reducing the risk of skidding.
AWD (All-Wheel Drive) A drivetrain that delivers power to all four wheels simultaneously, enhancing traction and stability.
Crossover A vehicle that combines characteristics of an SUV (Sports Utility Vehicle) and a passenger car.
CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission) A type of automatic transmission that seamlessly varies gear ratios, providing smoother acceleration.
Differential A device that splits torque between the wheels, allowing them to rotate at different speeds, essential for smooth turns.
Downforce The aerodynamic force that pushes a vehicle towards the ground, improving traction and stability at high speeds.
EV (Electric Vehicle) A vehicle that operates entirely on electric power, using one or more electric motors for propulsion.
Horsepower A unit of power measurement in the automotive context, indicating the engine's ability to perform work.
Hybrid A vehicle with both an internal combustion engine and an electric motor, allowing for improved fuel efficiency and reduced emissions.
OBD-II (On-Board Diagnostics II) A standardized system for monitoring and reporting vehicle performance issues, helping in diagnostics and maintenance.
Oversteer A handling condition where the rear tires lose traction during a turn, causing the rear of the vehicle to swing out.
PHEV (Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicle) A hybrid vehicle with a larger battery that can be charged by plugging into an external power source.
RWD (Rear-Wheel Drive) A drivetrain configuration where power is sent to the rear wheels, typically found in performance and sports cars.
Sedan A four-door passenger car with a separate trunk compartment for cargo.
SUV (Sports Utility Vehicle) A versatile vehicle designed for off-road capabilities with the comfort and amenities of a passenger car.
Torque The rotational force produced by an engine, important for a vehicle's acceleration and towing capabilities.
Traction Control System A system that regulates wheel spin during acceleration by adjusting engine power or applying brake force to specific wheels.
Turbocharger A device that increases engine power by forcing more air into the combustion chamber, improving efficiency.
Understeer A handling condition where the front tires lose traction during a turn, causing the vehicle to drift wide.
VIN (Vehicle Identification Number) A unique code assigned to each vehicle, containing information about its manufacturer, model, and specifications.
Wattage The power rating of a car's audio system, measured in watts, indicating its sound output capability.
Wheelbase The distance between the front and rear axles of a vehicle, affecting stability and ride comfort.
Xenon Headlights High-intensity discharge headlights that produce a brighter and more focused light compared to traditional halogen bulbs.
Zero-Emission Vehicle (ZEV) A vehicle that produces no tailpipe emissions, often referring to electric or hydrogen fuel cell vehicles.
2WD (Two-Wheel Drive) A drivetrain configuration where power is delivered to either the front or rear wheels, commonly found in smaller vehicles and trucks.
